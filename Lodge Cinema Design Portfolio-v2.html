Shiplap Walls

Specification: Horizontal Pine Shiplap · Dark Walnut Stain · Flat Matte Finish

Unfinished 23/32" × 7⅛" × 8ft pine shiplap planks, stained with Minwax Dark Walnut #2716 gel-stain process, finished with flat wipe-on poly. Installed horizontally across all non-brick framed walls. The stain must be preceded by wood conditioner on every board — pine blotches severely without it.

Material + Stain SpecsFinalized
BoardRELIABILT 23/32" × 7⅛" × 8ft Unfinished Pine Shiplap
ConditionerMinwax Pre-Stain Wood Conditioner — apply 15 min before stain
StainMinwax Oil Stain — Dark Walnut #2716
TopcoatMinwax Wipe-On Poly — Flat finish only
ApplicationLint-free shop rags — no brushes
DirectionHorizontal installation
Expected result~#5A4638 warm walnut tone

Critical: Stain before install whenever possible. Working horizontal boards on sawhorses gives even, consistent results. Staining vertical installed boards is significantly harder and results are less uniform.

Exact Process — Do In This OrderFollow Exactly
  • 1
    Sand all boards 120 grit → 150 grit, with the grain
  • 2
    Wipe dust with tack cloth, wait 15–20 min
  • 3
    Apply Minwax Pre-Stain Conditioner — full coverage with rag
  • 4
    Within 15 minutes — apply Dark Walnut stain with clean rag, long strokes with the grain
  • 5
    Wipe excess at 4–5 minutes for target depth. Test timing on scrap first
  • 6
    Cure 8 hrs. Second coat optional for deeper tone — no second conditioner needed
  • 7
    Apply one coat Wipe-On Poly Flat with rag. Cure 24 hrs before install

Trim System

Specification: Flat Modern Matte Black Trim · No Profiles · Shadow Gap at Ceiling

All trim is RELIABILT 1×4 Select S4S Pine painted matte black. No ogee profiles, no cap molding, no colonial shapes. The trim's job is architectural framing — clean flat faces, butt joints at corners, and a shadow gap (no crown molding) at the ceiling junction. You need more than 25 boards — see quantity note below.

Trim Locations + DimensionsFinalized
Screen wall header1×4 full width above screen — or stack 1×4+1×4 for deeper header presence
Screen vertical flanking1×4 floor-to-ceiling, both sides of screen
Screen surround box1×4 tight frame around screen perimeter
Baseboard (all walls)1×4 flat — 3.5" face, square edge, no cap
Window casings (×2 windows)1×4 all four sides each window
Door casings (×2 doors)1×4 three sides each door
Riser step nosing1×2 flat black cap (separate from 1×4 stock)
Shiplap-to-brick return1×4 return piece at transition
Ceiling treatmentShadow gap only — no crown molding

Quantity note: 25 boards at 200 linear feet is likely short. Baseboard alone (~90 LF perimeter) + screen wall flanking + casings + waste cuts = 220–240 LF estimated. Buy 30–35 boards to be safe. Returns are easy; running short mid-install is not.

Paint Process for Pine Trim → Matte BlackShellac Primer Required
  • 1
    Light sand with 150 grit — S4S boards are pre-surfaced but may have mill marks
  • 2
    Apply Zinsser BIN Shellac-Based Primer — 1 coat. This is mandatory for pine; blocks resin bleed that turns black trim yellow at knots over time
  • 3
    Sand primer with 220 grit after dry, wipe dust
  • 4
    Apply Sherwin-Williams Emerald Urethane Trim Enamel — Tricorn Black SW 6258, Flat sheen — 2 coats with 4" foam roller on faces, brush on edges
  • 5
    Install after second coat fully cures (24 hrs)

Built-in open shelving running from riser seat height to ceiling in the booknook alcove. 3–4 shelves with matte black steel brackets and dark-stained pine shelf boards. Under-shelf LED lighting (warm amber, 2700K) on each level. Shiplap continues behind the shelving as the back wall.

Aesthetic IntentDesign Goal

  • →

  • →

    Keep shelf depth consistent with the alcove depth — don't let shelves protrude into the main riser walkway

Projector Screen Valance Box

Specification: Enclosed Beam-Integrated Valance · Fully Hidden Motor · Removable Bottom Panel

The existing structural beam above the screen wall becomes the valance. A three-sided box clads the beam and drops 5 inches below the beam bottom (4" motor housing + 1" buffer). The bottom panel is removable for service access, with a slot opening precisely sized to the screen bar — not the full screen width. From any viewing angle, the motor housing is completely invisible. The screen appears to emerge from architectural structure.

Valance Box ConstructionFinalized
Box depthMatch beam depth — 5 inches, flush with beam face
Face drop5 inches below beam bottom (4" housing + 1" buffer)
Front face materialPine boards, primed with shellac, painted Tricorn Black flat enamel
SidesReturned flush to wall on both ends — no visible gap
Bottom panelRemovable — secured with rare-earth magnets or turn-button fasteners, no visible screws
Slot openingWidth of screen bar + ¼" each side — not full screen fabric width
Slot positionRear of bottom panel — screen hangs flush toward wall side
FinishSame matte black as all trim — continuous with screen surround
Visual resultScreen rod appears flush with box bottom when raised
Why This WorksDesign Logic
  • →
    Beam is already structural and already at the right position — you're cladding something that exists, not adding a floating element
  • →
    The front face of the box aligns with your 1×4 vertical flanking trim — the entire screen wall reads as one composed frame from top to bottom
  • →
    Removable bottom means no destructive access if the motor or limit switch ever needs adjustment
  • →
    Slot sized to screen bar only — when screen is lowered, the fabric fills the visual opening. When raised, the rod sits flush and invisible from seating distance

Ceiling Touch-Up

Specification: Spot Treatment Only · Do NOT Refinish · Preserve the Patina

The ceiling's natural variation — dark patches, lighter boards, grain differences — is the most valuable thing about it. It looks like a 100-year lodge ceiling because it essentially is. Only the white water-stain spots and stark sun-exposure discoloration need treatment. Everything else gets left entirely alone. Total work: 2–3 hours, ~$30 in materials.

Touch-Up ProcessSpot Treatment Only
  • 1
    Clean: Vacuum loose dust with brush attachment. Barely-damp rag wipe. Let dry 48 hrs
  • 2
    Sand white spots only: 150 grit on water-stain areas — abrade the white mineral deposit, not the wood. Don't flatten grain
  • 3
    Spot stain: Minwax Dark Walnut gel stain on damaged areas with small rag. Feather edges 3–4 inches outward — no hard lines
  • 4
    Blend check: View from 8 ft. If still reading lighter, second pass after 2 hr dry
  • 5
    Seal spots only: Flat wipe-on poly on treated areas. Do NOT coat the whole ceiling
Do NOT Do These ThingsHard No
  • ✗
    Full sand and restain — destroys the patina that took decades to develop
  • ✗
    Bleach or oxalic acid wash — irreversible severe uneven lightening
  • ✗
    Paint over with tinted primer — you lose all wood grain permanently
  • ✗
    Satin or gloss poly — ceiling looks like a bowling alley under projector light
